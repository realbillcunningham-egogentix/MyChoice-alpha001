# MyChoice — Product Principles

The durable commitments that govern how MyChoice is built. When a trade-off is unclear, these decide it.

1. **Clarity Over Control.** MyChoice helps families understand digital life, not police it. We favor transparency and shared understanding over enforcement and restriction.

2. **Signals Are Observations, Not Judgments.** A signal is a derived, neutral measurement of behavior. It describes *what is happening*; it does not label a child as good or bad, healthy or unhealthy.

3. **Agreements Interpret Signals.** Meaning comes from the family's agreement, not a platform default. The same signal can be fine for one family and a concern for another; the agreement decides.

4. **Every Meaningful Decision Must Be Auditable.** Disclosures, escalations, status assignments, and deletions are recorded as decisions (never content) so the system can be reviewed and trusted.

5. **Parents See Patterns Before Content.** Guardians see derived patterns, never raw content. Raw activity stays on the child's side of the boundary; visibility is governed independently of collection.

6. **The Child Must Receive Value.** The child is a first-class user, not a monitored subject. Every feature must offer them genuine self-awareness or benefit — not just reporting to a parent.

7. **Governance Evolves With Maturity.** Authority, visibility, and autonomy change over time (graduated autonomy), culminating in a clean child-to-adult transition with re-consent.

8. **Family Values Override Generic Defaults.** Catalog bands and AI guidance are starting points; a family's agreement always overrides generic thresholds.

9. **AI Supports Human Judgment.** The AI layer explains, summarizes, and suggests — it never diagnoses and never makes governance changes autonomously. Significant changes require human confirmation.

10. **Transparency Builds Trust.** Children, parents, and operators can see why the system did what it did. Trust is earned through explainability, not assumed through authority.
