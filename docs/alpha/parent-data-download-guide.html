MyChoice Alpha — How to Get Your Data

A short, honest guide for families helping us test MyChoice.

What we're testing

MyChoice is exploring a simple idea: the social media platforms your family uses every day already keep detailed records of that activity — and you have the legal right to download a copy. We're testing whether those downloads can be turned into something useful for families: a clear, private picture of digital life that helps you talk about it together.

In this alpha, we're learning two things: whether the files the platforms hand over actually contain what families need, and whether the process of getting them is something real families can manage. You're helping us answer both.

What we're asking you to do

Each platform has a "download your data" button (it's a legal requirement — they all have one, though some hide it better than others). We'll ask you to press that button on a few platforms, wait for the platform to prepare your file, download it, and pass it to us through the secure hand-off method we set up with you personally.

That's it. No passwords are ever shared with us, nothing is installed on your accounts, and you stay in control of every step. You are downloading your own data, from your own account, and choosing to share it with us.

Which platforms to request first

1

Instagram & Facebook (together)

Go to Meta Accounts Center → Download your information. Choose both accounts if you have them, then: All available information, date range All time, format JSON, media quality High.

Usually ready in minutes to a couple of days. The download link expires after about 4 days, so grab it promptly.

2

Google / YouTube

Go to takeout.google.com. Select at least YouTube and My Activity. Where it offers a format choice for history, pick JSON. Tip: Takeout can also deliver a fresh copy automatically every two months — handy if you want to set it and forget it.

Usually ready within hours to a day. One thing to check first: if YouTube watch history is paused on the account, the export will be empty for that period.

3

TikTok

In the TikTok app: Profile → ☰ → Settings and privacy → Account → Download your data. Choose Select all and format JSON.

Usually ready in 1–4 days. Important: TikTok's download link expires just 4 days after the file is ready — check back and download it right away.

4

Snapchat

Log in at accounts.snapchat.com → My Data. Select all categories and the full date range.

Usually ready within a week, often within a day or two. Download promptly when the email arrives.

Why JSON? Platforms usually offer a "pretty" version (HTML) and a "machine-readable" version (JSON). The JSON version is the complete one, and it's what our tools read. When in doubt, pick JSON.

When your export ZIP arrives

  • Download it right away — most links expire within days.
  • Don't unzip or edit it. We want the original file exactly as the platform produced it.
  • Keep it somewhere private on your own computer (not a shared family desktop folder, not a public cloud link).
  • Hand it off the way we agreed with you — each family gets a specific, personal hand-off method from us.

Please do not email raw export files — to us or anyone — unless we have specifically instructed you to for your hand-off. These files contain your private messages, your search history, and more. Email is not a safe place for them.

Being straight with you about the alpha

We'd rather over-explain than have you find out later:

  • Early testing happens in a closed, local data environment. Your files are processed on controlled, access-restricted machines used by the small MyChoice alpha team — not on a public website or a big cloud platform.
  • This is alpha software, not a finished product. Our security practices are careful but they are not yet production-grade — no 24/7 security team, no certified audits yet. That's exactly why we keep the environment small and closed, and why we ask you not to email files around.
  • You can ask us to delete your raw exports after processing — or at any time — and we will. Deletion is covered in detail in the consent document you'll sign.

Questions families ask us

What data is actually inside these files?
More than most people expect: posts you've viewed and when, things you've liked and searched for, your messages, your follower lists, login times and devices, and the interest categories the platform's algorithms have guessed about you. Part of what MyChoice does is simply show you what's in there.
Who sees it?
Only approved members of the small MyChoice alpha team, for the purposes spelled out in your consent document. We don't sell it, we don't publish it, and we don't pass raw files to outside parties.
Can I delete it?
Yes. You can request deletion at any time, and by default we delete raw export files once we've finished processing them, unless you've agreed to let us keep them longer. What we may keep are anonymized lessons learned — things like "exports from platform X don't include feature Y" — which contain no personal data.
Is this for monitoring kids, or something else?
Something else. MyChoice is built to help families understand and talk about digital life — not to surveil it. We're interested in patterns (like late-night scrolling or how varied a feed is), shown to the family, for the family. It's a mirror, not a camera.
Can my child participate?
Possibly, with care. A parent or guardian must consent in writing, the child should be told what's happening in language they understand, and we apply extra caution to any minor's data. Talk to us first — minors' participation is decided family-by-family in this alpha.
